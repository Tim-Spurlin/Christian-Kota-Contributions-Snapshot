Augmented Reality Glasses for Accessible Communication

This flagship accessibility project is a full conversation-assistance platform for people who are deaf or hard of hearing. Instead of forcing users to look down at a phone for captions, the glasses place real-time subtitles, speaker context, and helpful memory cues directly in the user‚Äôs line of sight. The result is a more natural social experience where eye contact, timing, and confidence are preserved.

Inclusive by DesignAssistive captions that protect dignity, eye contact, and conversational flow.
Multimodal ReliabilityAudio transcription and lip-reading collaborate to reduce missed words in noisy spaces.
Personal Memory LayerFace-linked profiles store meaningful context, reminders, dates, and follow-ups.

Core Experience: Live Subtitle Intelligence

The headset runs a dual-input understanding engine: live audio transcription and AI lip-reading inference. These channels are fused with confidence scoring so if one channel degrades‚Äîlike in a loud restaurant or windy street‚Äîthe other channel supports continuity. Subtitles are rendered in short, readable units and positioned near the active speaker to reduce eye travel.

  • Fusion captioning pipeline: Audio and lip-motion models are blended with weighted confidence for robust phrase reconstruction.
  • Latency-aware formatting: Captions stream in readable chunks with punctuation smoothing and contextual timing.
  • Speaker-anchored overlay: Text follows speaker position to minimize cognitive load and improve turn-taking.
  • Conversation continuity mode: System degrades gracefully when one modality is weak, avoiding abrupt transcript gaps.
  • Context carry-forward: Ongoing sentence state helps maintain coherence when speech overlaps or is interrupted.

Identity + Memory: AI That Supports Human Relationships

When known individuals are detected, the interface can show compact profile cards with names and relationship context. Over time, vector embeddings convert conversational details into semantic memory so users can quickly recall what matters: birthdays, medication notes, commitments, project deadlines, preferences, and important life events.

  • Face recognition with contextual labels: Identify who is speaking and surface relevant identity cues.
  • Embedding-based recall: Memory works by meaning, enabling fuzzy retrieval of previously shared details.
  • Living relationship timeline: Profiles are updated continuously with reminders, milestones, and personal updates.
  • In-view nudges: Gentle prompts appear only when contextually useful to avoid visual overload.
  • Personalization depth control: Users can tune how much profile information appears in real time.
