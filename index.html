My Story

A journey of resilience, growth, and continuous learning.

Summary: A pivotal moment in early childhood where the world suddenly felt different. The memory of September 11th became one of those defining events that shaped a young perspective on safety, change, and the complexity of the world.

Reflection: This early exposure to global events taught me that the world is interconnected and that change can happen in an instant. It planted seeds of awareness about resilience and the importance of understanding broader contexts beyond my immediate surroundings.

Summary: Experiences with Ms. Reba and Ms. Jeanie & Mr. Jackie introduced me to different family structures and daily routines. Each household taught me adaptability and the value of stability even in changing circumstances.

Reflection: Foster care showed me the strength of the human spirit and the impact of compassionate caregivers. I learned empathy by experiencing different perspectives and developed resilience through navigating uncertainty. These experiences taught me to appreciate small kindnesses and the importance of creating safe spaces for others.

Summary: Living with Aunt Connie and Dave brought structure, discipline, and a foundation of family values. Their guidance helped shape character through accountability and expectations that pushed me to grow.

Reflection: Discipline wasn't just about rules—it was about building resilience and learning that growth comes from discomfort. This period taught me the value of consistency, hard work, and how structure can be a form of care. I developed grit and learned that challenges are opportunities for personal development.

Summary: Teachers like Mr. Key, Ms. Saunders, Mr. Harp, and Mrs. West became mentors who saw potential and encouraged academic curiosity. Their investment in students went beyond curriculum to shaping character and aspiration.

Reflection: These educators taught me that teaching is about more than content—it's about inspiration and belief. They demonstrated how one person's encouragement can alter a trajectory. I learned the power of mentorship and began to understand my own capacity to impact others through patience and investment in their growth.

Summary: High school was a time of exploration—from fashion experiments to early entrepreneurship with dropshipping, learning Java programming, navigating relationships, and experiencing the excitement of first phones and independence.

Reflection: This period taught me about self-expression, risk-taking, and the value of trying new things. Business ventures showed me both success and failure, building entrepreneurial thinking. Learning to code opened a door to problem-solving and creativity. I developed social awareness through diverse relationships and learned that identity is something you actively shape.

Summary: Air Force training and intelligence work demanded discipline, precision, and teamwork. This chapter brought structure, purpose, and a deep sense of service to something larger than myself.

Reflection: Military service refined my ability to work under pressure, pay attention to critical details, and collaborate with diverse teams toward common goals. It reinforced values of duty, honor, and commitment while showing me the importance of adaptability and continuous learning in high-stakes environments.

Summary: Now focused on teaching technology to empower others, learning multiple languages to connect across cultures, and building communities where people can grow together and support one another.

Reflection: All previous experiences have led to this: a mission to give back through education, connection, and community building. Teaching technology combines my technical skills with the mentorship I received. Language learning reflects my curiosity and desire to understand different perspectives. Building community honors those who built one for me, creating spaces where others can find support, growth, and belonging.
